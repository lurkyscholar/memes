It came from a place of wanting to understand and communicate with my community from my perspective as an inside-outsider. I saw the social media sphere which nebulously orbited our high school as an ever-shifting artifact of our collective consciousness, self consciousness, pretentious, presumptions, and follies. I found the people around me enrapturing and incomprehensible. At the time, I took the meme-as-medium very seriously, and sought to make the people around me intelligible through this project. The account gained a thousand followers overnight. I built a notorious and self-sustaining online ecosystem. People like to look at their own reflection. After the first three posts, all of the memes were generated and sent in by the community. After a week, I distributed the login information to carefully chosen representatives from each grade. After three weeks, I was locked out of the account. It remains today a testament to my own curiosity and to the enduring unintelligibility of the teenage population of Berkeley High School.
